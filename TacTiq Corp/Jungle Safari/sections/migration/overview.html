The Scale of the Great Migration

The Great Migration is not just an event; it's a perpetual cycle of life and death that defines the East African landscape. Over two million wildebeests, zebras, and gazelles move in a clockwise circle across the Serengeti-Mara ecosystem.

1.2 Million

Wildebeests form the core of the migratory herds, accompanied by hundreds of thousands of zebras.

1000 Miles

The average distance covered by a single animal during the annual migration cycle.

A Constant Search for Water

The herds follow the rains. They aren't just wandering; they are searching for the nutrient-rich grasses that spring up after the rainy season. This relentless pursuit of greener pastures is what drives the movement northward towards the Maasai Mara.
