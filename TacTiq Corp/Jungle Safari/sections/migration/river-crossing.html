The Mara River Crossing

This is the most dramatic and dangerous phase of the Great Migration. As the herds reach the banks of the Mara River, they face two major threats: the steep, treacherous banks and the Nile crocodiles lying in wait.

Survival of the Fittest

The crossing is a scene of pure chaos. Dust clouds rise as thousands of animals plunge into the water. Only those with the strength to navigate the current and the luck to avoid the predators make it to the other side.

The Waiting Predators

Nile crocodiles, some reaching 16 feet in length, congregate at common crossing points. They can go months without a large meal, waiting specifically for this annual event. It is nature at its most raw and unforgiving.
