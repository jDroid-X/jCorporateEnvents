The Tree of Life: Baobab

The Baobab is one of Africa's most iconic and ancient trees. Known as the "Upside-Down Tree" because its branches look like roots growing into the sky, it can live for thousands of years.

Water Reservoir

The Baobab can store up to 32,000 gallons of water in its massive trunk, allowing it to survive extreme droughts.

Ecosystem Pillar

It provides birds, insects, and small mammals with food, water, and shelter through the harshest seasons.

Ancient Sentinels

Some Baobabs are believed to be over 3,000 years old. They have witnessed the rise and fall of civilizations and remain standing as silent guardians of the savanna.
