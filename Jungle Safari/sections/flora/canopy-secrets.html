Secrets of the Canopy

The jungle canopy is a world of its own, bustling with life that never touches the ground. In the dense rainforests of Central Africa, the canopy layers support millions of species of insects, birds, and plants.

Rare Flora

From parasitic orchids to giant ferns, the canopy is home to some of the world's most exotic and rare plant life. These plants have adapted to high humidity and limited sunlight in the lower layers.

The Lungs of Africa

These forests play a critical role in regulating the continent's climate. They act as carbon sinks and generate much of the rainfall that sustains the surrounding savannas.
