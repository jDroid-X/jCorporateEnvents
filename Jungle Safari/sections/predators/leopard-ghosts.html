Leopard: The Ghost of the Savanna

The leopard is the most versatile and elusive of the big cats. Unlike lions, leopards are solitary and fiercely territorial. Their spotted coat provides perfect camouflage in both the dappled light of the forest and the tall grass of the savanna.

Masters of Ambush

A leopard can drag a carcass twice its own body weight up a tree to protect it from scavengers like hyenas and lions. This incredible strength and climbing ability make them the undisputed masters of the arboreal world.

The Solitary Life

Living alone means every injury can be fatal. Consequently, leopards are cautious and avoid direct conflict whenever possible. They rely on their senses—especially their vision and hearing—to navigate their world unseen.
